Without Haste Arabic Notes

Basics

Arabic is read from Right To Left.

Alphabet

Letters have Isolated and Final, Medial, Initial forms.

Hijā’ī Order

Letters are grouped by similarity of shape.

ﺎ ا Alif (ranges from e in bet, to a in father)
ﺑﺒﺐ ﺐ Ba (like b in bet)
ﺗﺘﺖ ﺕ Ta (like t in tip)
ﺛﺜﺚ ﺙ Tha (like th in three - tongue starts between your teeth)
ﺟﺠﺞ ﺝ Jeem (like j or g, depending on region)
ﺣﺤﺢ ﺡ Hha (breathy ha)
ﺧﺨﺦ ﺥ Kha (phlegmy gha or kha)
ﺪ ﺩ Dal (like d in dip)
ﺬ ﺫ Thal (like th in other - tongue at back of top teeth)
ﺮ ﺭ Ra (like Spanish r)
ﺰ ﺯ Zay (like z in zip)
ﺳﺴﺲ ﺱ Seen (like s in sip)
ﺷﺸﺶ ﺵ Sheen (like sh in she)
ﺻﺼﺺ ﺹ Saad (emphatic s, like s in subtle)
ﺿﻀﺾ ﺽ Daad (emphatic db, like d in duh)
ﻃﻄﻂ ﻁ Toh (emphatic t, like t in bottle)
ﻇﻈﻆ ﻅ Thoh (emphatic th, like th in thy)
